Navigating Your CSE Meeting in District 24

Committee on Special Education (CSE) meetings in District 24, serving Maspeth, Middle Village, Ridgewood, Sunnyside, Woodside, and Elmhurst, can be daunting. Understanding who is at the table and addressing our district’s unique challenges can empower you to advocate effectively for your child.

Who's at the Table? Expect to see a CSE Chairperson (often a school psychologist or administrator), teachers, related service providers (like speech therapists or occupational therapists), and, most importantly, a Parent Member. This trained parent volunteer from the district acts as a valuable resource, offering support and guidance throughout the IEP process. Don't hesitate to connect with them before or after the meeting.

Addressing District 24's Specific Challenges: Our district faces significant overcrowding in many schools, which can impact the availability and quality of IEP services. Coupled with a large population needing bilingual support (Spanish, Chinese, Polish), it’s crucial to advocate for appropriate resources. Ensure your child’s IEP clearly outlines necessary accommodations, modifications, and specially designed instruction, specifically considering the district's resource constraints.

Key Questions to Ask:

  1. Given the current class sizes and school overcrowding, how will my child receive the individualized attention outlined in their IEP?
  2. What specific bilingual support (Spanish/Chinese/Polish) resources are available at this school, and how will my child's progress be monitored in both their native language and English?
  3. How will the school ensure my child receives the mandated related services (e.g., speech therapy, occupational therapy) within the limited space and staffing resources, and what is the plan if these services are disrupted due to overcrowding?

Remember, you are your child's strongest advocate. Be prepared, ask questions, and work collaboratively with the CSE team to develop an IEP that meets your child's individual needs within the context of District 24's unique circumstances.
