Navigating the CSE Meeting in District 15: Advocacy in Action

Your child's Committee on Special Education (CSE) meeting in District 15 is a crucial step in ensuring they receive the appropriate support and services. Understanding who sits at the table and how to effectively advocate for your child can make a significant difference.

Key Players at the Table

You will typically find these individuals at your child's CSE meeting:

  • CSE Chairperson: This individual facilitates the meeting, ensures procedural safeguards are followed, and guides the development of the IEP.
  • Parent Member: A fellow parent of a child with a disability within District 15. They can offer valuable insights and support based on their own experiences navigating the special education system.
  • Other Members: Including your child's teacher, a school psychologist, a special education teacher, and any related service providers (e.g., speech therapist, occupational therapist).

Addressing Specific Concerns in District 15

District 15 is known for its commitment to inclusion, but issues can arise, especially regarding Integrated Co-Teaching (ICT) placement waitlists. Many families also seek guidance navigating the complexities of gifted programs alongside IEP-mandated services. Dual services can be hard to navigate. It's important to advocate for your child to receive the maximum possible advantage of their talents and abilities in accordance with their IEP.

Questions to Ask at Your CSE Meeting

Come prepared to ask questions that clarify your child's needs and the school's plan to meet them. Here are a few examples:

  1. Regarding inclusion: "What specific strategies and supports will be implemented in the general education classroom to ensure my child can fully participate and access the curriculum alongside their peers?"
  2. Regarding ICT: "If an ICT placement is recommended but currently unavailable, what interim supports will be provided, and what is the estimated timeline for placement?"
  3. Regarding Gifted & IEP Dual Services: "How will my child's IEP goals be integrated with their gifted programming to ensure a challenging and supportive learning environment that addresses both their strengths and areas of need?"

Remember to document all interactions and decisions made during the CSE meeting. Proactive communication and informed advocacy are key to ensuring your child receives the best possible educational experience in District 15.
