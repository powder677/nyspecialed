Navigating the CSE Meeting in Freeport UFSD

Understanding the Committee on Special Education (CSE) process is crucial for families in Freeport UFSD, particularly given our vibrant bilingual community and commitment to inclusion. These meetings are designed to create an individualized education program (IEP) tailored to your child's needs.

Who's at the Table? Expect to see a team of individuals contributing to the IEP. Key members include:

  • The CSE Chair: This individual oversees the meeting, ensuring all participants have the opportunity to contribute and that the IEP adheres to legal requirements.
  • Parent Member: This member, often another parent of a student with disabilities within Freeport UFSD, can offer valuable support and insight into navigating the special education system. They provide a parent's perspective and can advocate on your behalf.
  • Your Child's Teacher(s): General education and/or special education teachers who work directly with your child.
  • School Psychologist/Special Education Staff: Who can provide assessments and interpret assessment data.

Addressing the Unique Needs of Freeport's Community: As a district serving a large bilingual community, the CSE process in Freeport UFSD should be sensitive to linguistic and cultural differences. It’s vital to ensure that assessments are conducted in your child's native language, if necessary, and that the IEP considers their cultural background. The effectiveness of inclusion programs within Freeport UFSD should be discussed in the context of your child's specific needs and how these programs will support their academic and social-emotional growth.

Questions to Ask: Don't hesitate to ask clarifying questions! Here are three examples:

  1. "How will the IEP address my child's specific language needs, considering they are learning in both English and [Child's Native Language]?"
  2. "Can you provide examples of how the recommended inclusion program will be adapted to support my child's learning style and individual goals?"
  3. "What data will be used to track my child's progress on their IEP goals, and how frequently will I receive updates?"

By actively participating and asking informed questions, you can ensure your child receives the best possible educational support within Freeport UFSD.
