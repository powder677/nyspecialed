Navigating the CSE Meeting in District 16: Focusing on D75 Referrals, Behavioral Supports, and Related Services

The Committee on Special Education (CSE) meeting is a crucial step in determining the best educational path for your child. Understanding the process, particularly regarding D75 referral patterns, behavioral supports, and related services, is key to ensuring your child receives appropriate support within District 16 (Central Bedford-Stuyvesant).

Who's at the Table?

The CSE meeting involves several key individuals. The CSE Chairperson facilitates the meeting, ensuring that all participants have a chance to contribute and that the discussion remains focused. A Parent Member, an experienced special education advocate familiar with District 16 resources, is also present to provide support and guidance to parents. Other attendees include your child’s teacher(s), school psychologist, and any specialists who have worked with your child. Most importantly, you, the parent, are a vital member of the team.

D75 Referrals, Behavioral Supports & Related Services

District 16 faces specific considerations regarding referrals to District 75 (citywide specialized programs). It's important to understand the data behind these referral patterns and the criteria used for recommendation. Beyond D75, exploring robust behavioral supports within the mainstream setting is paramount. This includes understanding the school's capacity for implementing positive behavior interventions and supports (PBIS). Equally critical is addressing related services such as speech therapy, occupational therapy, and counseling. Be prepared to discuss your child's needs and how these services can be effectively delivered to support their progress.

Questions to Ask at the CSE Meeting:

  • What data supports the recommendation for D75, and what less restrictive environments have been considered and attempted?
  • What specific behavioral support strategies are currently being implemented in my child's classroom, and how is their effectiveness being monitored?
  • What related services are available within the school setting, and what is the process for accessing them? How will progress in these services be measured and communicated to me?
