Student Rights & Discipline in District 25

In NYC's District 25 (Flushing, Whitestone, College Point, Beechhurst), student rights are paramount. Fair and equitable discipline is essential for a positive learning environment. This overview highlights key aspects of student discipline, focusing on Manifestation Determination Reviews and suspension advice.

Manifestation Determination Review (MDR)

If a student with an Individualized Education Program (IEP) faces a suspension exceeding 10 school days, a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) must be conducted. The MDR team, including parents, relevant school staff, and the student (when appropriate), reviews the student's IEP, behavior intervention plan, and all relevant information. The purpose is to determine if the behavior leading to the suspension was caused by or directly related to the student's disability, or if the suspension was a direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP. If either is true, the student cannot be suspended and the IEP must be revised to address the behavior.

Advice Regarding Suspensions in District 25

If your child is facing suspension, understand your rights! Request a clear explanation of the charges and evidence. Attend all hearings and bring any supporting documentation. For students with IEPs, ensure an MDR is scheduled and conducted thoroughly. If you believe the suspension is unjust or violates your child’s rights, contact the Office of Student Enrollment and Fair Student Funding at the NYC Department of Education or seek legal advice. Advocate for alternative disciplinary measures, such as restorative justice practices and behavioral interventions. Remember, early intervention and open communication with the school are crucial in resolving disciplinary issues effectively.
