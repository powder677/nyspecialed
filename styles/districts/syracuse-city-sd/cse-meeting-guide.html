Navigating the CSE Meeting in Syracuse City SD

Attending a Committee on Special Education (CSE) meeting in the Syracuse City School District can feel daunting, especially given the high percentage (89%) of high-needs students requiring Refugee/Immigrant bilingual evaluations, CPSE (Committee on Preschool Special Education) services, and robust behavior supports. Understanding the process and who’s present is key to advocating for your child.

Who Sits at the Table?

Typically, you'll find several key individuals at the CSE meeting. The CSE Chair facilitates the meeting, ensures adherence to legal requirements, and summarizes decisions. Crucially, as the Parent/Guardian, you are a vital member of the team. Your input, observations, and concerns about your child are paramount. Other attendees might include general education teachers, special education teachers, school psychologists, related service providers (e.g., speech therapists, occupational therapists), and a parent member representing the district.

Addressing Key Concerns

Given Syracuse City SD’s focus on Refugee/Immigrant students, ensure language barriers are addressed. Request bilingual evaluations conducted by qualified professionals fluent in your child’s native language. If your child is receiving or requires CPSE services, review their Individualized Education Program (IEP) goals and progress. Advocate for appropriate behavior supports, including positive behavior interventions and supports (PBIS), tailored to your child's individual needs and cultural background. Don’t hesitate to share any relevant information regarding your child's background or experiences that may impact their learning.

Questions to Ask:

  • What specific evidence supports the proposed IEP goals, especially considering my child's bilingual background and cultural experiences?
  • How will progress towards these goals be measured, and what alternative interventions are available if my child isn’t making sufficient progress?
  • Given the high percentage of high-needs students in Syracuse City SD, what resources are available to ensure my child receives individualized and culturally responsive support, especially concerning behavior management strategies?
