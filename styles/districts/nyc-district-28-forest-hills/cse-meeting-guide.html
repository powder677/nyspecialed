Navigating Your CSE Meeting in NYC District 28

Your child's Committee on Special Education (CSE) meeting in District 28 is a crucial step in ensuring they receive the appropriate educational support. It's important to understand the process and advocate effectively.

At the table, you'll find several key individuals. The CSE Chairperson facilitates the meeting, ensuring procedures are followed and decisions are made collaboratively. A dedicated Parent Member, another parent of a child with special needs in the district, is also present to offer support and a parent's perspective.

District 28 is a large and incredibly diverse district, presenting unique challenges, particularly in the areas of multilingual evaluations and supporting Indo-Caribbean bilingual learners. We recognize the importance of culturally and linguistically appropriate assessments. If your child is assessed, ensure the evaluations reflect their linguistic background and cultural experiences. Specifically, for our Indo-Caribbean students, it’s vital to understand how their heritage influences their language development and learning style. Given the district's size, equitable resource allocation for these specialized evaluations and support services is also a key concern.

To ensure your child's needs are adequately addressed, consider asking the following questions:

  1. How are the evaluations specifically tailored to account for my child's multilingual background and cultural experiences, particularly regarding Indo-Caribbean cultural nuances if applicable?
  2. What specific professional development is provided to staff to ensure culturally responsive and linguistically appropriate instruction and assessment practices for diverse learners, including those with Indo-Caribbean heritage?
  3. Given the large size of District 28, how are resources allocated to ensure equitable access to specialized services, such as bilingual support and culturally relevant interventions, across all schools?

By understanding the process and asking informed questions, you can actively participate in shaping your child's educational future within District 28.
