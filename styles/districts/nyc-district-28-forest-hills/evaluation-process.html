Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in District 28

Local Context: Serving a Diverse Community

District 28 in Queens, NYC, encompasses vibrant and diverse neighborhoods like Forest Hills, Rego Park, Jamaica, Kew Gardens, and Richmond Hill. Given our diverse population, especially a significant Indo-Caribbean community, our schools offer multilingual special education evaluations wherever possible. Navigating the system in a large district like ours can be challenging, but this guide aims to clarify the initial step: requesting a special education evaluation.

The Written Referral: Your First Step

The formal process begins with a written referral to the Committee on Special Education (CSE). This letter is crucial. It should clearly state your concerns about your child's academic, social-emotional, or physical development. Be specific! Include examples of the challenges your child is facing. For example, instead of saying "My child is struggling in reading," say "My child struggles to decode simple words and often guesses, particularly when reading aloud. They become easily frustrated and refuse to read after a short period." While a referral can come from a teacher or other professional, parents have the right to request an evaluation directly.

Send your written referral directly to the CSE Chairperson for District 28. You can usually find their contact information on the NYC Department of Education website or by contacting your child's school. Keep a copy of your referral for your records. Consider sending it via certified mail to ensure proof of delivery.

Important Timeline: The 60-Day Clock

Once the CSE receives your written referral, the clock starts ticking. The district has a maximum of 60 school days to complete the evaluation process, including determining eligibility and developing an Individualized Education Program (IEP) if your child is found eligible. Be proactive! Follow up with the CSE Chairperson if you haven't heard back within a reasonable timeframe. Remember, advocating for your child is paramount. This 60-day period can feel long, so be prepared to gather any supporting documentation that might be helpful to the evaluation team.
