Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Schenectady City SD

This guide provides information for families in Schenectady City School District (SCSD) who believe their child may need special education services. SCSD is dedicated to serving all students, including those with high needs, refugee families, and English Language Learners (ELLs). We understand that navigating the special education process can be complex, and this resource aims to provide clarity.

Local Context: Is Your Child Struggling in School?

Schenectady City SD serves students from diverse neighborhoods, including Hamilton Hill, Mont Pleasant, Bellevue, and Vale. If you live in these or other areas of Schenectady and notice your child struggling academically, socially, or behaviorally, it's important to explore whether a special education evaluation is warranted. SCSD is committed to providing appropriate support for every student.

Making a Written Referral for Evaluation

To formally request a special education evaluation for your child in Schenectady City SD, you must submit a written referral to the Director of Special Education. This referral should clearly state your concerns about your child's learning or development. Be as specific as possible, providing examples of the challenges you've observed at home or in school. Include your child's name, date of birth, address, and the school they attend. Also, include your contact information (phone number and email address) so the district can reach you.

For bilingual evaluations, please clearly state your language preference in the referral letter. SCSD is committed to providing evaluations in your child's native language, as needed.

Important Timeline: The 60-Day Clock

Please be aware that once Schenectady City SD receives your written referral, the district has a maximum of 60 school days to complete the evaluation process. This includes conducting the evaluation, holding a Committee on Special Education (CSE) meeting to discuss the results, and determining eligibility for special education services. Actively participate and communicate with the school during this period to ensure the process moves efficiently.

CSE Advocacy and Support

We encourage you to learn about your rights as parents throughout this process. Consider seeking support from advocacy organizations that can assist you in understanding the CSE process and advocating for your child's needs. The Director of Special Education at Schenectady City SD can provide resources and further information.
