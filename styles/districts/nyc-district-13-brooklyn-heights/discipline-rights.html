Student Rights & Discipline in District 13

District 13 is committed to providing a safe and supportive learning environment for all students. This means upholding both student rights and ensuring fair disciplinary procedures.

Understanding Manifestation Determination Reviews (MDR)

If a student with an IEP or 504 plan faces a suspension exceeding 10 school days, a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR) must be conducted. This review, which includes parents/guardians, relevant school staff, and sometimes the student, determines if the student's behavior was a direct result of their disability or the school's failure to properly implement their IEP or 504 plan. If the behavior is deemed a manifestation of the disability, the IEP/504 team will need to revise the plan or provide additional supports to address the behavior. Suspensions may not be appropriate if the behavior is a manifestation.

Advice Regarding Suspensions in District 13

If your child is facing suspension in District 13, understand your rights. You have the right to be informed of the charges against your child and to present your side of the story. Document everything – dates, times, witnesses, and statements. If your child has an IEP or 504 plan, ensure the MDR process is followed correctly. If you disagree with the suspension or MDR outcome, you have the right to appeal. Contact your school's Parent Coordinator or the District 13 Community Education Council for assistance in navigating the appeals process. Seek legal advice if you feel your child's rights have been violated.
