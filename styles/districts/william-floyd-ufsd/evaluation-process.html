Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in William Floyd UFSD

Local Context: Serving Brookhaven's Military Families and Beyond

William Floyd Union Free School District (UFSD), serving the communities of Mastic, Shirley, and surrounding areas in Suffolk County, Long Island, is committed to providing quality special education services. We understand the unique challenges faced by all our families, including those relocating to our district from Brookhaven National Laboratory and our valued military families. Navigating the special education system can be complex, especially with the nuances of a more rural Long Island environment. This guide outlines how to request a special education evaluation.

The Written Referral: Your First Step

The formal process for requesting a special education evaluation begins with a written referral. This referral should be addressed to the Director of Special Education or your child's school principal. While verbal requests are important for initial discussions, a written referral formally triggers the evaluation process. In your letter, clearly state your concerns about your child's academic, social-emotional, or behavioral performance and how it impacts their ability to learn. Include specific examples whenever possible. Mention any interventions that have already been tried and the outcomes. The more information you provide, the better the Committee on Special Education (CSE) can understand your concerns.

  • Key elements of your referral letter:
  • Child's full name and date of birth
  • School and grade level
  • Your specific concerns (with examples)
  • Any interventions attempted
  • Your contact information

Important Timeline: The 60-Day Clock

Be aware that William Floyd UFSD, like all districts in New York State, has a 60-school-day timeline from the date of your written referral to complete the evaluation and hold the initial CSE meeting. While the district strives to adhere to this timeline, unforeseen circumstances may occasionally cause delays. It is crucial to submit your written request as soon as you have concerns to allow ample time for the evaluation process. Regular communication with the school throughout this process is highly encouraged.

For further assistance or clarification, please contact the Director of Special Education.
