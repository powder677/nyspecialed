Navigating Your CSE Meeting in District 19

The Committee on Special Education (CSE) meeting in District 19, covering East New York, Brownsville (east), and Starrett City, is a crucial step in ensuring your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) effectively addresses their needs. Understanding the process and key players is essential for a productive meeting.

Who's at the Table? You'll find several important individuals present. This includes the CSE Chairperson, responsible for facilitating the meeting and ensuring adherence to regulations. The Parent Member serves as a neutral advocate for parental rights and can provide guidance throughout the process. Teachers, related service providers (like therapists or counselors), a school psychologist, and often a district representative will also be present, alongside you, the parent or guardian.

Key Concerns in District 19: Be prepared to discuss specific issues that commonly arise within the district. These often include related service gaps, such as limited access to occupational therapy, speech therapy, or counseling services, impacting students' progress. School safety and Behavior Intervention Plans (BIPs) are another vital area, especially when a student's behavior interferes with their learning or the learning of others. Finally, manifestation determination hearings occur when a student with an IEP faces disciplinary action, requiring the CSE to determine if the behavior was a manifestation of their disability. Understanding your child's rights during these hearings is crucial.

Questions to Ask: To ensure your child's needs are being met, consider asking these questions:

  • What specific strategies will be implemented to address the documented gaps in related services, and how will the effectiveness of these strategies be measured?
  • Can you provide data on school-wide safety initiatives and how these initiatives specifically support students with IEPs who may be vulnerable or experiencing behavioral challenges?
  • In the event of a disciplinary incident, what proactive measures are in place to prevent future occurrences, beyond reactive consequences, and how do these align with my child's BIP?

By actively participating and addressing these key areas, you can effectively advocate for your child's success within the CSE process in District 19.
