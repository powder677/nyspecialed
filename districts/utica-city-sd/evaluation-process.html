Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Utica City School District

Local Context: Understanding Your Child's Needs

Utica City School District (UCSD) is committed to supporting all students, especially given the unique needs of our diverse community. With approximately 85% of our students identified as high-needs, and a significant refugee population residing in areas like Cornhill, East Utica, and West Utica, providing appropriate special education services is paramount. It's crucial that evaluations accurately reflect a child’s strengths and challenges, and for many of our students, that means bilingual evaluations.

Initiating the Evaluation Process: The Written Referral

The first step in requesting a special education evaluation for your child is to submit a written referral to the Utica City School District. This letter should clearly state your concerns about your child's academic, social-emotional, or behavioral development and how these concerns are impacting their school performance. Be as specific as possible, including examples and observations from home and school. You should send this letter to your child's school principal, guidance counselor, or directly to the Director of Special Education (contact information available on the UCSD website).

Your referral should include:

  • Your child's full name and date of birth
  • Your child's school and grade level
  • Your contact information (phone number and email address)
  • A detailed description of your concerns
  • Any supporting documentation (e.g., previous evaluations, doctor's notes)
  • If applicable, a request for a bilingual evaluation

Important Timeline: Be Aware of the 60-Day Limit

Once the school district receives your written referral, they have a legal obligation to respond. Be aware that UCSD has a 60 school day timeline (excluding summer months and school breaks) to complete the evaluation and hold a Committee on Special Education (CSE) meeting to discuss the results and determine eligibility for special education services. It is essential to keep track of this timeline and proactively communicate with the school if you have any concerns or haven't heard back within a reasonable timeframe. Delays can impact your child's access to needed support.
