Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in NYC District 75

This guide outlines the process for requesting a special education evaluation for your child within New York City's District 75, which serves students with significant disabilities across all five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island. Whether you reside in Flushing or Flatbush, Riverdale or Richmond Hill, this information applies to you. Understanding your rights is crucial to navigating this process effectively.

Local Context: District 75 provides specialized programs and schools throughout the city, including programs like ACES, Horizon, and NEST. These programs cater to diverse learning needs. If you believe your child requires the unique supports offered by District 75, such as intensive academic instruction, specialized therapies, or travel training, initiating a referral is the first step.

The Written Referral: Your First Step

The process begins with a written referral submitted to the Committee on Special Education (CSE) Chairperson at your child's current school or zoned school if your child is not yet enrolled. This referral letter should clearly state your concerns regarding your child's academic, social-emotional, or physical development. Provide specific examples of challenges your child faces and why you believe a special education evaluation is necessary. Be as detailed as possible.

Remember to include your contact information (phone number and email address) and your child’s full name, date of birth, and current grade (if applicable). It is also helpful to mention any previous evaluations or interventions your child has received.

Important: The 60-Day Timeline

Be aware that the school district has 60 school days from the date they receive your written referral to complete the evaluation and hold a CSE meeting to determine your child's eligibility for special education services. It is critical to track this timeline. If the district does not meet this deadline, you have the right to file a complaint. Know your placement rights and don't hesitate to advocate for your child's needs throughout this process. Contact the CSE Chairperson for any questions or clarification.
