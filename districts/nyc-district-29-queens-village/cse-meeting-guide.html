Navigating Your CSE Meeting in District 29

Attending a Committee on Special Education (CSE) meeting in District 29, which serves neighborhoods like Cambria Heights and Rosedale, can feel overwhelming. Understanding the process and advocating effectively for your child's needs is crucial. Remember, the goal is to create an Individualized Education Program (IEP) that best supports your child's success.

Who's at the Table? Expect to see several key individuals. The CSE Chairperson leads the meeting, ensuring procedures are followed. A Parent Member is another parent from the district with experience in special education, there to offer support and guidance. Other attendees include your child's teachers, related service providers (like therapists), a school psychologist, and potentially your child (depending on their age and abilities). You, as the parent or guardian, are the most important member.

District 29 has a diverse population, and specific concerns often arise. For families requiring Caribbean bilingual evaluations, ensure the evaluator is culturally competent and understands the child's specific dialect and language background. Discuss the rationale behind SETSS (Special Education Teacher Support Services) vs. ICT (Integrated Co-Teaching) placement – what specific learning environment is best suited for your child's individual needs and learning style? Also, start planning for transition services early, especially for older students. This includes exploring vocational training, college preparation, and independent living skills.

Here are three key questions to consider asking during your CSE meeting:

  • Based on the bilingual evaluation, what specific supports and accommodations are recommended to address my child's language and learning needs in the classroom?
  • What data supports the recommendation for SETSS/ICT placement? How will progress be monitored in this setting?
  • What transition planning services are available to help my child prepare for life after high school, and when should we start exploring these options?

Remember to document everything discussed and request a copy of the IEP promptly. By understanding the process and being prepared with specific questions, you can actively participate in creating an effective IEP for your child.
