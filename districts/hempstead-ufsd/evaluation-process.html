Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Hempstead UFSD

If you believe your child residing within the Hempstead Union Free School District (UFSD) needs special education services, you have the right to request a special education evaluation. This guide provides information on how to initiate this process.

Local Context

Hempstead UFSD serves a diverse population across various neighborhoods in Hempstead, Nassau County, including areas around Clinton Street Elementary, Prospect School, and the communities near Hempstead High School. Understanding the unique challenges faced by students in our high-poverty suburban setting is crucial. Advocates are available to assist families navigate this process.

The Written Referral: Your First Step

To begin the special education evaluation process, you must submit a written referral to the Director of Special Education at Hempstead UFSD. This letter should clearly state your concerns about your child's academic or developmental progress and why you believe a special education evaluation is necessary. Include specific examples of difficulties observed at home or in school. Be sure to include your child's full name, date of birth, address, grade, and school.

  • Address your letter to: Director of Special Education, Hempstead UFSD
  • Clearly state: "I am requesting a special education evaluation for my child, [Child's Full Name]."
  • Briefly describe: Your specific concerns about your child's learning.
  • Provide contact information: Phone number and email address where you can be reached.

Important Timeline: The 60-Day Rule

After the district receives your written referral, they have 60 school days to complete the evaluation and hold an Individualized Education Program (IEP) meeting to discuss the results. This is a crucial timeline. Monitor the process closely and follow up with the Director of Special Education if you haven't received updates within a reasonable timeframe. Proactively communicating can help ensure the evaluation proceeds without unnecessary delays. Remember that this timeline is from the date the district receives your written referral, not the date you mail it.
