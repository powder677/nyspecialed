Navigating Your CSE Meeting in District 7

Understanding the Committee on Special Education (CSE) process is crucial for advocating for your child's needs in District 7, which encompasses the South Bronx, Mott Haven, Melrose, Port Morris, and Hunts Point. The CSE meeting is where your child's Individualized Education Program (IEP) is developed and reviewed.

Who's at the Table?

Key players at the CSE table include:

  • The CSE Chairperson: Oversees the meeting, ensures proper procedures are followed, and facilitates discussion. This person represents the NYC Department of Education.
  • Parent Member: A trained parent volunteer who provides support and guidance, and can offer an informed perspective on navigating the special education system.
  • You, the Parent/Guardian: The most important voice! You know your child best.
  • Other Professionals: This may include teachers, therapists, school psychologists, and other specialists relevant to your child's needs.

Addressing Key Concerns in District 7

District 7 faces specific challenges, particularly regarding related services shortages, CPSE (Committee on Preschool Special Education) delays, and D75 referrals (referrals to specialized schools for students with significant needs). Don't hesitate to directly address these concerns during your meeting. Be prepared to present evidence of your child's needs and how these issues are impacting their progress. If you are experiencing delays in receiving services or concerns about a D75 referral, bring those concerns to the CSE's attention. Document everything, including dates of requests and communications.

Questions to Ask:

  1. What specific strategies are in place to address the shortage of [Therapy type - e.g. Speech therapy] services in District 7, and how will those strategies impact my child's access to timely support?
  2. What is the current average wait time for CPSE evaluations and placement in District 7, and what steps are being taken to expedite this process for children transitioning from early intervention?
  3. Can the CSE clearly outline the criteria used to determine if a D75 referral is the most appropriate placement for my child, and what alternative options were considered?

Remember, you are your child's best advocate. Prepare, be informed, and don't hesitate to ask questions to ensure your child receives the appropriate support and services.
