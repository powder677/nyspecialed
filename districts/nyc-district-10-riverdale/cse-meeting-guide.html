Navigating Your CSE Meeting in District 10

The Committee on Special Education (CSE) meeting is a crucial step in ensuring your child receives appropriate educational support. In District 10, covering neighborhoods like Riverdale, Kingsbridge, and Fordham, it's important to be prepared and advocate effectively. Understanding who's present and how to address your concerns will make the process smoother.

Who's at the Table?

Expect to see several individuals at your child's CSE meeting, including:

  • CSE Chairperson: This individual leads the meeting, ensuring it stays on track and facilitating discussion. They represent the NYC Department of Education.
  • Parent Member: A trained parent volunteer who provides support and understanding based on their own experience with special education. They can offer valuable insights and perspectives.
  • Representatives from your child's school (teachers, administrators)

Addressing Key Concerns: ICT, Related Services, and Gifted with IEP

Specifically addressing ICT classroom placements, related services, and navigating a gifted IEP requires careful planning. If you're seeking an Integrated Co-Teaching (ICT) classroom, clearly articulate why you believe it's the best placement for your child, highlighting the benefits of collaborative teaching. Regarding related services (speech therapy, occupational therapy, etc.), ensure the IEP accurately reflects the frequency and duration of services required. If your child is identified as gifted but also requires an IEP, advocate for enrichment activities and differentiated instruction that cater to their advanced abilities while addressing their learning needs. Remember that the IEP should be individually tailored to your child.

Questions to Ask During the Meeting:

  1. What data or assessments support the proposed IEP goals, particularly regarding [specific area of concern, e.g., reading comprehension]?
  2. How will my child's progress in the ICT classroom (or related service, or gifted programming) be monitored and reported to me?
  3. If [specific placement/service] is not available at my child's current school, what alternative options are being considered and what are the steps involved in exploring those options?
