Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in Buffalo City SD

Local Context: Knowing Your Community

Buffalo City School District (BCSD) proudly serves diverse neighborhoods throughout Buffalo, including the West Side, East Side, North Buffalo, and South Buffalo. As a high-needs district, with approximately 84% of students qualifying for free or reduced lunch, BCSD is committed to providing comprehensive special education services to all eligible students. We understand the unique challenges faced by our community and strive to offer support tailored to individual needs.

The Written Referral: Getting the Process Started

To request a special education evaluation for your child in Buffalo City SD, you must submit a written referral. This referral should clearly state your concerns about your child's academic, social-emotional, or behavioral development. Include specific examples and any supporting documentation, such as report cards, teacher notes, or medical diagnoses. Address the referral to the Director of Special Education at the BCSD central office. While verbal communication with school staff is helpful, a formal, written request is essential to initiate the evaluation process. Many bilingual evaluations are conducted. Please specify in your letter if you need this to be in another language.

Important Timeline: Understanding the 60-Day Limit

Please be aware that Buffalo City SD has a legal obligation to complete the special education evaluation within 60 school days of receiving your written referral. This timeline includes the initial evaluation, Committee on Special Education (CSE) meeting to determine eligibility, and development of an Individualized Education Program (IEP) if your child is found eligible. While the district strives to adhere to this timeline, factors such as scheduling challenges or the need for specialized evaluations can sometimes cause delays. It is important to communicate regularly with the school psychologist and CSE chairperson to stay informed about the progress of the evaluation. Be proactive and contact the Director of Special Education if you have concerns about the timeline.

Remember that transition services begin at age 15. If your child is this age and has not had an IEP review, please contact us immediately.

Contact Information

For further assistance, please contact the Director of Special Education at Buffalo City SD.
