Requesting a Special Education Evaluation in District 9

Local Context: Where We Are

This guide is for families in District 9, which encompasses the Grand Concourse, Highbridge, Morrisania, and Tremont neighborhoods of the Bronx. If you believe your child is struggling academically, behaviorally, or socially-emotionally and needs specialized support, understanding the process for requesting a special education evaluation is crucial.

Initiating the Evaluation: A Written Referral

The first step is to submit a written referral to the Committee on Special Education (CSE) Chairperson for District 9. This referral must clearly state your concerns about your child's educational performance. Be specific! Include examples of challenges your child faces in the classroom or at home that you believe impact their learning. Common areas of concern for District 9 families include difficulty with focus, emotional regulation, and social skills leading to classroom disruptions. Many parents are concerned with Behavioral IEP goals.

Your written referral should be sent via certified mail to ensure proof of delivery. Keep a copy for your records.

Key Areas for IEPs in District 9: Behavioral Goals and Protections

  • Behavioral IEP Goals: Ensure your child's IEP includes specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) goals addressing problematic behaviors. Advocate for positive behavioral interventions and supports (PBIS).
  • Suspension Protections: Understand your child's rights regarding suspension. Students with IEPs have specific protections.
  • Manifestation Determinations: If your child is facing disciplinary action (e.g., suspension), a "manifestation determination" meeting must be held to determine if the behavior is related to their disability. This is critical!

Important Timeline: The 60-Day Clock

Be aware that the school district has a maximum of 60 school days from the date they receive your written referral to complete the evaluation process and hold an IEP meeting. Track this timeline carefully. If you haven't heard back within a reasonable timeframe, contact the CSE Chairperson for District 9 to follow up.

For additional information, please contact the District 9 CSE Chairperson.
